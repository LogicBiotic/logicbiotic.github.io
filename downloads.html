MendozaH_Thesis Manuscript FINAL SIGNED.pdf

For my undergraduate thesis, I examined the effects of urbanization on stream ecosystems by analyzing over 18,000 benthic macroinvertebrate specimens from waterways in and around UP Diliman. Using both taxonomic identification and functional trait analysis (feeding type, respiration, mode of reproduction, & locomotion), I assessed community responses across land use types and seasons. Results showed significant seasonal shifts in diversity and tolerance scores, while functional traits remained stable, indicating a uniformly stressed aquatic environment. The project involved all stages of research, from design and fieldwork to data analysis using QGIS, RStudio, and PAST.
